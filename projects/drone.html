U.S. Army Drone Design Competition (2019)

The 2019 U.S. Army Drone Design Competition was the inaugural HBCU/MI (Historically Black Colleges and Universities/Minority Institutions) Design Competition, hosted by the University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP) in April. Eleven teams competed by designing, building, and demonstrating drones that addressed challenges posed by the U.S. Army's Combat Capabilities Development Command (CCDC).

  • What it was: The first-ever U.S. Army HBCU/MI Design Competition, focusing on student innovation in unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) technology.
  • Where: Sun Bowl Stadium, El Paso, Texas.

  • Who competed: 11 student engineering teams from Historically Black Colleges and Universities and Minority Institutions.
  • What we did: Designed and piloted a biologically-inspired drone chassis using additive manufacturing, integrating lightweight sensors and control systems to optimize performance in obstacle navigation.
  • Results: Placed 2nd in Design and 4th nationally overall.
  • Extra events: Poster showcase, technology expo, and networking with Army researchers.
